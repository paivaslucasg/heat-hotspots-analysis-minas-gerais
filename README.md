# heat-hotspots-analysis-minas-gerais
This project investigates the spatial distribution of heat hotspots in the state of Minas Gerais using exploratory spatial analysis techniques and spatial econometric modeling. The central objective is to identify geographic patterns, regional groupings and possible effects of spatial dependence associated with the occurrence of fires. Hot spots represent complex socioeconomic factors, resulting from climate, land use and territorial particularities. Therefore, the study of these phenomena requires a spatial analysis of the data, considering that the occurrence of these events between neighboring regions does not occur independently.
# Motivation
The literature shows that hot spots have strong spatial autocorrelation, since fires can spread to neighboring regions, these neighboring regions have similar climatic conditions and activities related to land use tend to be spatially concentrated in specific locations. 
Authors such as Anselin (1988) and LeSage & Pace (2009) emphasize the importance of spatial analysis to avoid biased estimates and misinterpretations. Furthermore, hot spots are highly associated with economic activities that involve land use (Nepstad et al., 2001; Morton et al., 2008).
